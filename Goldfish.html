CHAPTER 1

Welcome, Eilidh & Finlay! 🎉

Congratulations on your very first goldfish! Goldfish are friendly, colourful pets that can live for a long time — some even live 15-20 years — if you take good care of them.

🛒 What You'll Need Before Your Fish Arrives

  • A big tank — at least 75-100 litres for one goldfish
  • A filter — to keep the water clean
  • Gravel or sand
  • A water conditioner
  • A water testing kit
  • A fish net
  • Fish food — goldfish flakes or pellets
  • Decorations — nothing sharp!

🐠 A Big Important Fact!

Goldfish grow to be 15-30cm long! They need lots of room to swim. A tiny bowl is never enough space — see Chapter 4 for why.

CHAPTER 3

Meet the Goldfish Family 🐟

Common Goldfish

Classic orange, single tail. Hardy and fast-growing — needs a very large tank or pond!

Comet Goldfish

Slim, speedy, long flowing tail. One of the fastest swimmers!

Fantail Goldfish

Round and chubby, cute double tail. Swims slower and gently.

Shubunkin

Speckled rainbow of blue, orange, black & white. Every one is unique!

Oranda

Famous bubbly "hat" on its head. Gentle and slow-moving, so keep tankmates calm too.

CHAPTER 4

Why Bowls Aren't Happy Homes 🚫🥣

You might see a goldfish in a little round bowl at a fair — but that's not a happy home. Here's why:

🏊

Not enough room to swim

🧪

Water gets dirty quickly

💨

Very little oxygen

🌡️

Hard to keep steady temperature

It's About Kindness, Not Rules 💛

A spacious, clean tank means a happier, healthier fish that can live a long life with you.

CHAPTER 5

Setting Up Your Tank 🏠

1. Pick the Right Size

At least 75-100 litres for one goldfish. Bigger is always better!

2. Rinse Everything

Rinse tank, gravel, and decorations with plain water — no soap!

3. Fit a Filter

Goldfish make lots of waste, so a good filter is a must.

5. Keep it Cosy, Not Hot

18-22°C is ideal. Keep away from sunlight and radiators.

4. Fill With Treated Water & "Cycle" the Tank

Add a water conditioner, then wait. Don't add your fish until water tests show the tank is fully cycled — this depends on tiny bacteria growing, not the calendar, and usually takes several weeks. See Chapter 6!

CHAPTER 6 · SCIENCE BIT 🔬

The Tiny Invisible Helpers

The most important secret in fishkeeping — let's tell it like a story!

🐟 Goldfish make wee and poo, just like any other pet.
That creates ammonia — poisonous to fish! 😱
⬇️
Millions of tiny helpful bacteria live in your filter and gravel — working hard all day, even though you can't see them!
⬇️
🐟 Fish waste → Ammonia 😱 → Nitrite 😬 → Nitrate 🙂
⬇️
When you change some water each week, the nitrate goes away too. Never rinse your filter under the tap — you'd wash away the helpers!
CHAPTER 7

Keeping the Water Healthy 💧

📅

Every Day

Check your fish and the water look clear and happy.

🧪

Every Week

Test the water (ask a grown-up). Wipe algae off the glass.

🚿

Every Week or Two

Change 10-20% of the water with conditioned water.

🧹

Every Month

Rinse the filter gently in old tank water only.

⚠️ Never Do This!

  • Never change 100% of the water at once
  • Never use soap or chemicals in the tank
  • Never rinse your filter under the tap
CHAPTER 8

Feeding Your Goldfish 🍽️

How Much & How Often

Flakes or sinking pellets, 1-2 times a day — what your fish can finish in 2 minutes.

✅ Good Treats

Peas, broccoli, spinach, bloodworms, daphnia, brine shrimp

🤏 Sometimes

Cucumber, lettuce

❌ Never

Bread, crackers, cereal, cheese

🚫 The Golden Rule

"When in doubt, don't feed!"

CHAPTER 9

Is My Goldfish Happy? 😊

✅ Happy Fish

  • Exploring & swimming
  • Begging for food
  • Fins open
  • Bright colour

⚠️ Stressed Fish

  • Hiding a lot
  • Clamped fins
  • Sitting in a corner
  • Gasping at surface

💛 Things Goldfish Like

Hiding places · clean water · gentle current · exploring · routine · getting to know you!

CHAPTER 10

Can My Goldfish Have Friends? 🐠🐠

✅ Usually Good

Other goldfish, especially similar size and speed.

🤔 Advanced Only

Hillstream or weather loaches — need very experienced keepers.

❌ Usually Avoid

Tropical fish, tiny fish (may become a snack!), aggressive fish.

Goldfish are happiest with other goldfish that swim at the same speed! 🐟💫

CHAPTER 11

Plants For Your Tank 🌿

🌿 These Usually Survive

Anubias, Java Fern, and Hornwort are tough enough for a nibbling goldfish.

😋 These Become Lunch!

Softer, leafy plants often get eaten fast — that's just your fish enjoying a tasty snack!

CHAPTER 12

Seasons & Your Goldfish ❄️☀️

Cooler water makes goldfish slower and less hungry — totally normal, just like some animals slow down in winter!

Tell the Difference

A slower fish in cold weather is usually fine. But if it stops eating for many days or hides constantly, check the Emergency chapter!

CHAPTER 13

Can Goldfish Learn? 🧠

Yes! Goldfish can:

  • Recognise the faces of people who feed them
  • Learn feeding time
  • Follow a moving finger
  • Learn to be hand-fed

🌟 Try This!

Feed from the same corner every day — soon your fish may swim over as soon as it sees you!

PARENT CORNER

A Quick Reference for Grown-Ups

The rest of this guide is written for the kids — this page is for you.

Ammonia: Toxic fish waste product; should read 0ppm once established.
Nitrite: Toxic intermediate stage of the nitrogen cycle; also 0ppm once cycled.
Nitrate: End product, far less toxic; controlled with regular partial water changes.
GH/KH: Water hardness — worth testing if pH seems unstable.
Dechlorinator: Neutralises chlorine/chloramine — use on every water change.
Fishless cycling: Establishing bacteria before adding fish — the kinder method.
Quarantine: Separate tank for new arrivals for 2-4 weeks.
Test kits: Liquid master kits are more accurate long-term than strips.

Fancy varieties (Oranda, Fantail) are more delicate swimmers and shouldn't be mixed with fast single-tailed varieties (Common, Comet).
